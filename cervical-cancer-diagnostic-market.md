<p><p>The cervical cancer diagnostic market includes various types of tests and procedures. Pap testing is a commonly used method that involves collecting cells from the cervix to detect any abnormalities. HPV testing is another method that checks for the presence of human papillomavirus, a leading cause of cervical cancer. Colposcopy is an examination that uses a special instrument to closely examine the cervix. Cervical biopsies involve removing a small sample of cervical tissue for further analysis. Cystoscopy is a procedure that examines the bladder and urethra for signs of cancer.</p></p>
