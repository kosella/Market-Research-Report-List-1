<p><p>Coagulants are commonly used chemicals in water treatment processes to remove impurities and suspend particles. Inorganic coagulants, such as aluminum sulfate and ferric chloride, work by destabilizing the contaminants and forming dense flocs that can be easily removed. Organic coagulants, like polyamines and polyDADMAC, are derived from natural or synthetic polymers and are effective in treating organic matter. Additionally, there is another category of coagulants that includes various types such as composite coagulants and non-metal coagulants, which offer alternative solutions for water treatment depending on specific needs and requirements.</p></p>
