<p><p>A fiber-coupled acousto-optic modulator (AOM) is a device used in optics and photonics that enables the modulation of laser beams through the interaction of sound waves and light waves. It consists of a crystal through which a laser beam is passed and a piezoelectric transducer that generates sound waves. The interaction between the sound waves and the laser beam changes the intensity, frequency, or phase of the laser beam, allowing for various applications such as laser beam steering, modulation, and switching.</p><p>The fiber-coupled acousto-optic modulator market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The Fiber-coupled Acousto-optic Modulator (AOM) market can be categorized into two types: Single-Mode (SM) Fiber and Sub-Miniature-Polarization-Maintaining (SMPM) Fiber. SM Fiber AOMs use a single-mode optical fiber to couple the acoustic and optical waves, enabling efficient modulation of light signals. On the other hand, SMPM Fiber AOMs are designed specifically for polarization-maintaining fibers, ensuring stability and reliability in polarized light applications. Both types of AOMs offer unique benefits, making them suitable for various optical communication and laser systems.</p></p>
