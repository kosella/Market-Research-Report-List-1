<p><p>Copper ion selective electrodes are devices used to measure the concentration of copper ions in a solution. There are two types of electrodes commonly used, liquid membrane and PVC membrane. Liquid membrane electrodes consist of an organic liquid membrane that is selectively permeable to copper ions. PVC membrane electrodes, on the other hand, contain a polyvinyl chloride matrix that is impregnated with a copper-ion sensitive material. Both types of electrodes are effective in accurately detecting and quantifying copper ions in various samples.</p></p>
