<p><p>The Large Aperture Scintillometer market involves the use of two types of lenses: Plano-convex Lens and Fresnel Lens. A Plano-convex Lens has a curved surface on one side and a flat surface on the other, allowing it to converge incoming light. On the other hand, a Fresnel Lens is composed of concentric grooves on both sides, enabling it to focus light like a traditional lens but with reduced thickness and weight. These lenses play a crucial role in the scintillation measurement process of large aperture scintillometers.</p></p>
