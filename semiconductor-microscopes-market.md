<p><p>Semiconductor microscopes are used for examining microscopic structures in semiconductor materials. There are three main types of semiconductor microscopes available in the market. Optical microscopes use visible light to magnify the sample and are commonly used for routine semiconductor inspection. Near-field probe microscopes use a sharp probe to study the sample's surface with high resolution. Electron microscopes utilize a beam of electrons to image the sample, providing extremely detailed information about its structure. These microscopes assist semiconductor manufacturers and researchers in analyzing and understanding the properties and quality of semiconductor materials.</p></p>
