<p><p>Bioresorbable polymers are materials that can be gradually broken down and absorbed by the body. There are several types in the market, including polylactic acid (PLA), polyglycolic acid (PGA), polysaccharides, polycaprolactone (PCL), and poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA), among others. PLA is derived from renewable resources such as cornstarch and is commonly used in medical implants and drug delivery systems. PGA is a synthetic polymer with excellent mechanical properties, making it suitable for applications like sutures. Polysaccharides, including chitosan and gelatin, are natural polymers used in tissue engineering and wound healing. PCL is a biodegradable polyester that finds applications in drug delivery and tissue engineering. PLGA is a copolymer of PLA and PGA, known for its biocompatibility and controlled release properties. Each type of bioresorbable polymer offers unique properties that make them suitable for various medical applications.</p></p>
