<p><p>R134A refrigerant is produced using two different processes: the Tetrachlorethylene Process and the Trichlorethylene Process. The Tetrachlorethylene Process involves the reaction of trichloroethylene with hydrogen fluoride to produce R134A. On the other hand, the Trichlorethylene Process involves the reaction of 1,1,1-trichloroethane with hydrogen fluoride to produce R134A. Both processes are used to create R134A refrigerant, which is widely used in air conditioning and refrigeration systems due to its low environmental impact and favorable thermodynamic properties. These processes ensure the availability of R134A for various applications in the market.</p></p>
