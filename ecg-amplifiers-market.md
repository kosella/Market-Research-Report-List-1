<p><p>ECG amplifiers are devices used to measure and amplify the electrical signals produced by the heart. They are available in various market types including 2-channel, 8-channel, 16-channel, 32-channel, 64-channel, 128-channel, and 256-channel. Each of these market types refers to the number of channels, or input leads, that the amplifier can simultaneously process. For example, a 2-channel ECG amplifier can record signals from two leads, while a 128-channel amplifier can capture signals from 128 leads. Essentially, these market types indicate the varying levels of multi-lead capabilities and scalability options for recording and analyzing the heart's electrical activity.</p></p>
