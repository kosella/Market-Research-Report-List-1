<p><p>White sugar is a widely consumed sweetener that is derived from two main sources, cane sugar and beet sugar. Cane sugar is extracted from sugarcane plants, mainly grown in tropical regions. It is known for its large crystals and slightly caramelized taste. On the other hand, beet sugar is obtained from sugar beet plants, commonly cultivated in temperate climates. It has smaller crystals and is recognized for its neutral flavor. Despite their differences, both cane sugar and beet sugar are essential components of the white sugar market, catering to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
