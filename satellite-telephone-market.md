<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Satellite telephone market types are characterized by their orbital positioning. Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite telephone systems consist of a network of satellites placed in orbits closer to the Earth's surface, offering lower latency, faster connectivity, and global coverage. On the other hand, Geosynchronous (GEO) satellite telephone systems involve stationary satellites positioned in geostationary orbit above the equator, enabling them to remain fixed relative to the Earth's surface. This provides broader coverage areas but higher latency due to the longer distance the signal must travel.</p></p>
