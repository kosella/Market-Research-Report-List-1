<p><p>Polymer Light Emitting Diodes (PLEDs) have two main types of markets: Passive Matrix and Active Matrix. The Passive Matrix PLED market involves a simple addressing scheme where each pixel is individually controlled. It is cost-effective but limited in resolution. The Active Matrix PLED market utilizes a transistor for each pixel, enabling precise control and higher resolution. It offers better image quality but is more expensive. Both markets cater to the demand for PLED displays, with Passive Matrix being suitable for simpler applications and Active Matrix for more advanced and high-resolution applications.</p></p>
