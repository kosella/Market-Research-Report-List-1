<p><p>Artificial joint market encompasses various types such as ceramics, alloys, oxinium, and others. Ceramics artificial joints are made of biocompatible materials like alumina or zirconia, providing durability and smooth movement. Alloy artificial joints are created using metals like stainless steel or cobalt-chromium, ensuring robustness and stability. Oxinium artificial joints combine ceramic and metal properties, offering enhanced wear resistance. Additionally, the market includes various other types, including polyethylene or ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene (UHMWPE) joints, providing flexibility and durability. These diverse options enable healthcare professionals to choose the most suitable artificial joint based on a patient's individual needs.</p></p>
