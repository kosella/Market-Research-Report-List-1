<p><p>Metallic biocides are commonly classified into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic metallic biocides are composed of carbon-based compounds, such as quaternary ammonium compounds, phenolic compounds, and organosulfur compounds. Inorganic metallic biocides, on the other hand, are composed of metal-based compounds like silver, copper, and zinc. Organic metallic biocides are often favored for their low toxicity and broad spectrum of activity, while inorganic metallic biocides are preferred for their durability and stability in various environmental conditions.</p></p>
