<p><p>An inverted bucket steam trap is a type of steam trap used in steam systems. It operates on the principle of condensate depressurization. The trap consists of a bucket inside a cylindrical body, which is connected to the steam system. When steam enters the trap, it fills up the bucket, causing it to sink down and open the valve. This allows condensate to be discharged from the system. As the steam condenses, the bucket becomes buoyant and rises, closing the valve and preventing steam from escaping.</p><p>The market for inverted bucket steam traps is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
