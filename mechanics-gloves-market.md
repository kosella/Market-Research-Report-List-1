<p><p>Mechanics gloves are designed to provide protection and improve grip for individuals working with tools or machinery. There are three main types of mechanics gloves: PVC coated, latex coated, and uncoated. </p><p>PVC coated gloves are manufactured with a layer of polyvinyl chloride, making them resistant to oil, grease, and chemicals. These gloves are ideal for heavy-duty tasks that require maximum durability and protection.</p><p>Latex coated gloves are made with a layer of natural latex, offering excellent grip and flexibility. They are suitable for tasks that involve handling small parts or delicate objects.</p><p>Uncoated gloves do not have any additional coating, allowing for enhanced breathability and dexterity. These gloves are commonly used for light-duty tasks that require precision and sensitivity.</p><p>Overall, the choice of mechanics gloves depends on the specific needs of the user, considering factors such as the type of work, comfort, and level of protection required.</p></p>
