<p><p>Passenger Car Energy Recovery Systems include two main types: Automotive Regenerative Braking System and Automotive Waste Heat Recovery System. The Automotive Regenerative Braking System converts the kinetic energy produced during braking into electrical energy, which is then stored in a battery or capacitor for later use. On the other hand, the Automotive Waste Heat Recovery System captures and utilizes the waste heat generated by the engine and exhaust system to generate electricity or provide additional power, improving overall fuel efficiency and reducing emissions.</p></p>
