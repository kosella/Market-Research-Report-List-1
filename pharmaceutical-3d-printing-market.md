<p><strong>The Pharmaceutical 3D Printing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM)</li><li>Melt Extrusion Deposition (MED)</li><li>Powder Binding (PB)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Stereolithography (SLA)</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pharmaceutical 3D printing market encompasses various types of printing technologies. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) involves melting a plastic filament and then depositing it layer by layer to create an object. Melt Extrusion Deposition (MED) method uses a melted material that is extruded through a nozzle and solidifies rapidly to form the desired shape. Powder Binding (PB) employs a liquid binder to fuse powdered material together, creating a solid object. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) uses lasers to fuse powdered material together. Stereolithography (SLA) utilizes a laser to solidify liquid resin layer by layer. Other types of printing technologies are also present in the pharmaceutical 3D printing market.</p></p>
