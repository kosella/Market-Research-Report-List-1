<p><p>A bariatric walker is a mobility aid designed specifically for individuals who are obese or have a higher weight capacity. It provides support, stability, and balance while walking, helping them to maintain their mobility and independence. Bariatric walkers often come with wider frames, heavy-duty construction, and reinforced components to accommodate the weight and size of the user.</p><p>The market for bariatric walkers has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. The increasing prevalence of obesity worldwide is one of the key factors driving market growth. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the global obesity rate has tripled since 1975, with nearly 2 billion adults overweight or obese in 2016. The rising awareness about the importance of staying active and mobile among individuals with obesity has also contributed to the demand for bariatric walkers.</p><p>In addition, technological advancements in the design and features of bariatric walkers have propelled market growth. Manufacturers are focusing on incorporating innovative features such as height adjustability, foldability, and ergonomic handles to enhance the user experience. The introduction of lightweight and portable bariatric walkers has also attracted a wider customer base.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing geriatric population, which is more prone to mobility issues and obesity-related conditions, is expected to drive the demand for bariatric walkers in the coming years.</p><p>The bariatric walker market is anticipated to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.4% during the forecast period.
