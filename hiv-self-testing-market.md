There are three main market types in HIV self-testing: blood, oral fluid, and urine. Blood-based tests, such as rapid blood tests, detect HIV antibodies in a blood sample. Oral fluid tests use saliva to detect antibodies, while urine tests detect viral antigens or antibodies in urine samples. These different testing methods cater to varying user preferences and offer convenience and privacy for individuals who want to test themselves for HIV.</p></p>
