<p><p>Optical preclinical imaging refers to a non-invasive imaging technique that utilizes light to visualize and measure biological structures and functions in small animal models. It enables researchers to study and understand diseases and therapies at the molecular and cellular level in a live animal setting before translating to human applications. Optical preclinical imaging techniques include fluorescence imaging, bioluminescence imaging, photoacoustic imaging, and others.</p><p>The optical preclinical imaging market is experiencing significant growth. The market growth can be attributed to several factors, including increasing research and development activities in life sciences, growing demand for non-invasive imaging techniques, and advancements in technology. Additionally, the rise in the adoption of optical imaging in drug discovery and development, personalized medicine, and diagnostics is driving market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing focus of pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies on preclinical testing and early-stage drug development is also boosting the demand for optical preclinical imaging systems. These imaging systems aid in the evaluation of drug efficacy, safety, and pharmacokinetics, reducing the reliance on animal experimentation.</p><p>The market is witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory.
