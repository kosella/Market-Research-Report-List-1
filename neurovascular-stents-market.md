<p><p>Neurovascular stents refer to medical devices used to treat narrow or blocked blood vessels in the brain. Carotid artery stents are specifically designed to treat blockages in the carotid arteries, which supply blood to the brain. Intracranial stents, on the other hand, are used to treat blockages within the blood vessels inside the brain itself. These stents are inserted through minimally invasive procedures to help restore blood flow, prevent strokes, and improve patient outcomes in neurovascular diseases.</p></p>
