AC piezoelectric motors are designed to operate using alternating current, where the polarity of the voltage periodically changes. On the other hand, DC piezoelectric motors use direct current, which maintains a constant voltage polarity. Both types offer unique advantages in terms of precision, compactness, and fast response, making them suitable for applications requiring precise positioning, such as robotics, microelectronics, and medical devices.</p></p>
