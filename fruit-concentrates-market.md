<p><p>Fruit concentrates are available in two main forms: liquid concentrate and powder concentrate. Liquid concentrate is made by removing the water content from fruit juice to achieve a thicker consistency, while powder concentrate is obtained through the dehydration of liquid concentrate. Liquid concentrates are usually used in beverages, such as ready-to-drink fruit juices or smoothies, while powder concentrates are commonly utilized in the production of powdered drink mixes or as an ingredient in bakery products. Both forms provide convenience and longer shelf life compared to fresh fruit.</p></p>
