<p><p>A non-fungible token (NFT) represents a unique digital asset that cannot be exchanged on a like-for-like basis due to its distinctive characteristics. NFTs are typically built using blockchain technology, which ensures transparency, security, and immutability of ownership.</p><p>The market for NFTs has experienced explosive growth in recent years, driven by increased interest from artists, collectors, and investors. The market value of NFTs surged from just $41 million in 2018 to over $2 billion in 2020, representing a significant increase. NFTs have gained attention primarily in the art and gaming industries, where they offer new opportunities for creators to monetize their digital creations and for collectors to acquire unique and scarce assets.</p><p>One of the key drivers of the NFT market's growth is the rise of online marketplaces dedicated to buying and selling NFTs. These platforms provide a convenient and secure environment for creators and collectors to transact and have contributed to the mainstream adoption of NFTs. Additionally, the increasing integration of NFTs into popular culture, with celebrities and influencers endorsing and creating their own NFTs, has further fueled market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the NFT market has witnessed the emergence of new trends such as fractional ownership, where multiple individuals can have fractional shares in a single NFT, expanding access and liquidity.
