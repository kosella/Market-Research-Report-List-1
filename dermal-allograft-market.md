Cellular allograft involves the use of skin tissue that contains living cells, such as fibroblasts, while acellular allograft refers to the use of skin tissue that has had its cells removed, leaving behind the extracellular matrix. Both types have applications in various medical procedures, such as wound healing and tissue repair.</p></p>
