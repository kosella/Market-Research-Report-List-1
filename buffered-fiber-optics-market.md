<p><p>Buffered fiber optics refer to the type of fiber optic cables that have a protective layer, called a buffer, applied to the individual optical fibers within the cable. This buffer provides added protection against physical damage and environmental factors. The buffered fiber optics market includes two main types: indoor and outdoor fiber optics. Indoor fiber optics are designed for use within buildings and offer high bandwidth connectivity for various applications. Outdoor fiber optics, on the other hand, are specifically designed to withstand harsh environmental conditions and are commonly used for long-distance communication and connectivity between buildings or across different geographical areas.</p></p>
