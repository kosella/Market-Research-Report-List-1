<p><ul><li>Secondary Packaging</li><li>Primary Packaging</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The alcohol packaging market consists of two main types: secondary packaging and primary packaging. Secondary packaging refers to the protective packaging used outside the primary packaging, such as cartons, cases, or wraps, which protects the bottles during transportation and storage. On the other hand, primary packaging refers to the direct packaging of the alcohol products, such as glass bottles, cans, or pouches, which not only safeguard the contents but also serve aesthetic and marketing purposes. Both secondary and primary packaging are crucial for ensuring the safety, freshness, and visual appeal of alcohol products in the market.</p></p>
