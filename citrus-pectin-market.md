<p><p>Citrus pectin is a natural substance derived from citrus fruits and widely used in various food and pharmaceutical applications. It is classified into two types: high methoxyl pectin (DM: more than 50) and low methoxyl pectin (DM: less than 50). High methoxyl pectin is mainly used for making jellies and gummies due to its ability to form a gel when combined with sugar and acid. On the other hand, low methoxyl pectin is used in products that require controlled gel strength or to stabilize protein-based foods, such as yogurts and dairy desserts.</p></p>
